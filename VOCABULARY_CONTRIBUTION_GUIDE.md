# iTárók App — Vocabulary Contribution & Verification Guide

## The Problem We Are Solving

The initial vocabulary in this app was generated by an AI that inferred Tarok words from related Benue-Congo language patterns. Your in-house Tarok speaker has correctly identified that many words are wrong. **This guide explains how to fix them properly and ensure it never happens again.**

---

## How the Data Is Structured

Every word in `data/tarok.js` now has a `confidence` field:

| Level | Meaning | Action needed |
|---|---|---|
| `"confirmed"` | Found in Longtau (2008), NBTT publications, or the official Tarok alphabet chart | ✅ Safe to publish |
| `"traceable"` | Word appears in a book title, example sentence, or academic footnote | 🔍 Native speaker should confirm |
| `"unverified"` | AI-estimated from Benue-Congo language patterns | ❌ Must be replaced before publishing |
| `"placeholder"` | Slot for a word we know we need but don't have yet | ❌ Must be filled by expert |

---

## How Your In-House Expert Should Submit Corrections

### Option A — Direct edit (fastest)
1. Open `data/tarok.js`
2. Find the word to correct (search by English meaning)
3. Replace the `tarok` and `ipa` fields with the correct values
4. Change `confidence` to `"confirmed"`
5. Add `sourceNote: "Verified by [name], [date]"`

**Example — correcting a greeting:**
```js
// BEFORE (placeholder)
{
  tarok: "⚠️ NEEDS EXPERT INPUT",
  english: "Hello / Welcome",
  ipa: "—",
  confidence: "placeholder",
}

// AFTER (corrected by expert)
{
  tarok: "Aagun",           // ← correct Tarok word
  english: "Hello / Welcome",
  ipa: "/àa.gùn/",         // ← IPA with tones marked
  confidence: "confirmed",
  sourceNote: "Verified by [Expert Name], March 2026. Also: Longtau 2008 p. XX",
  notes: "General greeting used any time of day",
}
```

## How to Get More Verified Data — Recommended Sources

### 🥇 Tier 1 — Primary (most reliable)

1. **Longtau, S.R. (2008). The Tarok Language: Its Basic Principles and Grammar.**
   Kay Williamson Educational Foundation. Jos: DART.
   - The definitive grammar of Tarok. Contact KWEF Nigeria or University of Jos library.
   - Email: kwef@kwef.net or University of Jos library

2. **Lar, M.N., Longtau, S.R. & Bendor-Samuel, P. (1994). A Trilingual Tarok Dictionary.**
   Nigeria Bible Translation Trust, Jos.
   - Contact NBTT Jos: nbttjosoffice@yahoo.com

3. **Tarok New Testament (Nsar Nnap-nla Inan Mpipye)**
   Nigeria Bible Translation Trust, 1988.
   - Available from the Nigeria Bible Translation Trust

### 🥈 Tier 2 — Academic papers (peer-reviewed)

4. **Onah, P. & Israel, T. (2022). A Phonological Description of Tarok.**
   Journal of English and Communication in Africa, 5(3&4):1–24.
   - Available at: jecaoauife.com (the URL you shared; try direct PDF link)

5. **Blench, R. & Longtau, S.R. (2012). A Tarok Gender Register in Context.**
   - Available on Academia.edu (free account required)

6. **Longtau, S.R. (1993). A Formal Tarok Phonology.** Afrika und Übersee, 76:15–45.

### 🥉 Tier 3 — Community & Digital

7. **The Tarok Language Committee** (Langtang)
   - The official body for Tarok orthography and language policy
   - Based in Langtang, Plateau State; contact through Langtang LGA secretariat

8. **University of Jos — Department of Languages and Linguistics**
   - Has several Tarok-focused theses and can connect you with native speaker linguists
   - Contact: linguistics@unijos.edu.ng

9. **Nigeria Bible Translation Trust (NBTT), Jos**
   - Has the most extensive corpus of written Tarok (New Testament, readers, alphabet materials)
   - Jos office: +234 (0)8033... (call University of Jos for current number)

10. **SIL International — Nigeria**
    - Has legacy Tarok linguistic materials; may share wordlists
    - sil.org/areas/africa/nigeria

### 🎙️ Tier 4 — Oral / Community Recording

11. **Langtang Community in Diaspora**
    - Tarok diaspora associations exist in Lagos, Abuja, and UK. LinkedIn groups exist.
    - Useful for rapid verification of common words

12. **Record a native speaker directly**
    - Best approach: Record a fluent Tarok speaker (ideally from Langtang-North/South)
    - Use IPA notation or audio transcription tools like ELAN (free software from MPI)
    - Aim for at minimum: greetings, numbers 1–20, body parts, family, food, common verbs

## IPA & Tone Marking Guide for Contributors

Tarok is tonal. When submitting words, please mark tones:

- High tone: acute accent → á, é, í, ó, ú (voice rises)
- Low tone: grave accent → à, è, ì, ò, ù (voice falls)
- Mid tone: no mark (normal pitch)

**Example:**
- `nàa` = cattle/cow (low tone)
- `náa` = mother (honorific) (high tone)
- These are DIFFERENT words — tone is essential!

## What NOT to do

❌ Do not use Google Translate — Tarok is not supported  
❌ Do not infer Tarok words from Hausa or Igbo — these are different language families  
❌ Do not use AI-generated Tarok words without expert verification  
❌ Do not copy vocabulary from uncited websites — errors spread quickly
